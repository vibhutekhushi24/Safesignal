SAFETY GUIDE

Step-by-step emergency protocols. Available even without internet.

🔍
🔥
Fire Emergency Protocol ▼
  • 1
    Activate the nearest fire alarm immediately and call 101.
  • 2
    Alert everyone in the building. Do NOT use elevators.
  • 3
    Crawl low under smoke — air is cleaner near the floor.
  • 4
    Feel doors before opening — if hot, use alternate exit route.
  • 5
    Meet at designated assembly point and await fire services.
🌍
Earthquake — Drop, Cover, Hold ▼
  • 1
    DROP to hands and knees immediately.
  • 2
    Take COVER under a sturdy desk, table, or against an interior wall.
  • 3
    HOLD ON until shaking stops. Protect your head and neck.
  • 4
    After shaking: check for gas leaks, exit carefully via stairs only.
  • 5
    Stay away from damaged buildings. Expect aftershocks.
🌊
Flood Response Actions ▼
  • 1
    Move immediately to higher ground — never wait for official evacuation orders.
  • 2
    Do NOT walk or drive through floodwater — 15cm can knock you down.
  • 3
    Turn off electricity at the main breaker before water enters.
  • 4
    Secure important documents in waterproof bags. Take emergency kit.
  • 5
    Call NDRF Helpline: 1078 for rescue assistance.
🩺
CPR — Adult First Aid ▼
  • 1
    Check responsiveness — tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?"
  • 2
    Call 108 (Ambulance) immediately or have someone call while you begin CPR.
  • 3
    Place heel of hand on centre of chest. Interlock fingers.
  • 4
    Push hard and fast — 100-120 compressions per minute, 5–6cm depth.
  • 5
    After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if trained. Repeat cycle.
🛡️
Women's Personal Safety ▼
  • 1
    Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, act immediately.
  • 2
    Call Women Helpline 1091 or Nirbhaya Helpline 181.
  • 3
    Share live location with trusted contact using SafeSignal's Live Location feature.
  • 4
    Move toward crowded public places, avoid isolated areas.
  • 5
    Use the SOS button to trigger a silent alert to your emergency contacts.
🚗
Road Accident Response ▼
  • 1
    Call 108 for ambulance and 100 for police immediately.
  • 2
    Do NOT move injured persons unless there is immediate danger (fire/flooding).
  • 3
    Turn on hazard lights. Place warning triangle 50m behind vehicle.
  • 4
    Control bleeding with firm pressure using clean cloth.
  • 5
    Keep injured persons warm and conscious until help arrives.

01:04 AM
Performing CPR: **Step 1:** Call 108 (Ambulance) for help, even if you're unsure about the victim's condition. **Step 2:** Check the victim's airway, breathing, and circulation (ABCs). - **Airway:** Tilt head back, lift chin up. - **Breathing:** Listen for breath sounds, feel for breath on your cheek. - **Circulation:** Check for a pulse in the neck. **Step 3:** Start CPR if the victim shows no signs of breathing and has no pulse. - **30 Chest compressions:** Place your hands in the center of the chest, press down to 2-3 inches, release. - **2 Rescue breaths:** Pinch victim's nose shut, give one breath lasting 1 second. **Step 4:** Continue 30:2 ratio - compressions: breaths until help arrives. **Important:** CPR should be performed by someone trained in it. If not certified, call 108 for assistance. Remember: Every second counts, act swiftly.
